Popular Destinations

Goa

Goa is India's smallest state, located on the west coast, famous for its stunning beaches, Portuguese heritage (colonial architecture, churches), vibrant culture, and tropical climate, with tourism, mining, and agriculture as key industries, known for its Konkan cuisine, rich biodiversity (Western Ghats), and attractions like Dudhsagar Falls and historic Old Goa

Coorg

Coorg (Kodagu) is a picturesque hilly district in Karnataka's Western Ghats, famous for its coffee plantations, spice gardens, waterfalls, rich wildlife, and unique Kodava culture, offering pleasant weather from October to March for tourists exploring places like Madikeri, Abbey Falls, and Raja's Seat, making it the "Scotland of India". Key Highlights.

Kerala

Kerala, a state on India's tropical Malabar Coast, has nearly 600km of Arabian Sea shoreline. It's known for its palm-lined beaches and backwaters, a network of canals. Inland are the Western Ghats, mountains whose slopes support tea, coffee and spice plantations as well as wildlife. National parks like Eravikulam and Periyar, plus Wayanad and other sanctuaries, are home to elephants, langur monkeys and tigers.

Dandeli

Dandeli, located in North Karnataka's Uttara Kannada district, is a premier eco-tourism and adventure hub famous for the dense forests of the Kali Tiger Reserve, thrilling activities like whitewater rafting, kayaking, and jungle safaris, and natural spots like the Kavala Caves and Sathodi Falls, attracting nature lovers and adrenaline seekers alike. Known as a "Green City," it offers rich biodiversity, teak plantations, and cultural experiences, with the Kali River flowing through, making it a year-round destination, especially from October to March for pleasant weather
